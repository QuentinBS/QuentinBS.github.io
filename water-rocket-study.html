Water Rocket Design & Launch Study

This project is structured as a developing experiment focused on propulsion, flight stability, and how design changes affect performance. It is intended to connect classroom physics to measurable launch results through repeated testing and clearer documentation.

View project log Read first article

Study purpose

The project is intended to examine how design and launch conditions affect a simple rocket system in flight. It should create a clear record of changes, observations, and measurable outcomes.

Physics connection: connect forces, pressure, motion, and energy transfer to a visible launch system.
Design comparison: compare how build variables influence height, stability, and distance.
Documentation habit: develop stronger project records through repeated trials and structured notes.

Tracked variables

Water volume inside the rocket.
Air pressure used for launch.
Fin design, including shape, angle, and symmetry.
Rocket mass and overall balance.

Measured outcomes

Maximum height

Used to compare overall launch effectiveness between configurations.

Total flight time

Useful for understanding ascent, descent, and overall flight behaviour.

Horizontal distance

Helps show angle drift, imbalance, and aerodynamic inconsistency.

Flight stability

Qualitative observations can show wobble, spin, and directional control issues.
